Could You Have Prediabetes?

Do you have any family history of diabetes?

A family history of diabetes could contribute to your risk for type 2 diabetes.

Have you ever been diagnosed with high blood pressure?

Having high blood pressure contributes to your overall risk for type 2 diabetes.

What race or ethnicity best describes you?

People of certain racial and ethnic groups are more likely to develop type 2 diabetes than others.



Are you physically active?

Being inactive can increase your risk for type 2 diabetes.

Have you ever had unusual weight gain or large weight fluctuations in recent years?

Significant weight fluctuations can be a sign of metabolic disorders and may indicate an increased risk of type 2 diabetes.

Frequently asked questions
  • What is Prediabetes Risk Test and why is it important?
    Pre-diabetes testing is an onine assessment used to determine if a person is at risk of developing type 2 diabetes in the near future. It is important because it can provide early warnings and allow interventions to prevent or delay the development of diabetes, but it does not replace a visit to a specialist doctor.
  • Who should have a pre-diabetes test?
    Anyone who has risk factors for type 2 diabetes should consider a pre-diabetes test. These risk factors include family history of diabetes, obesity, lack of physical activity and unhealthy diet.
  • What are the possible results of a pre-diabetes test?
    The test results may indicate a low, medium or high risk of developing type 2 diabetes. Depending on the answers, the chances are calculated, but they are not always accurate, so a visit to a medical specialist is necessary.
